SIP Calculator with Inflation & Step-Up SIP

Plan your SIP investments with real-world accuracy using inflation-adjusted future value, step-up SIP (yearly increase in contributions), and a goal-based reverse SIP calculator.

See both nominal corpus and actual purchasing power so you do not confuse a big number on screen with what that money can really buy in future.

How This SIP Calculator Works

The SIP calculator uses the standard SIP formula \( FV = P \times \frac{(1 + r)^n - 1}{r} \times (1 + r) \) where P is the monthly SIP, r is monthly return, and n is the number of months.[web:227][web:233]

The inflation-adjusted purchasing power is calculated by dividing the future value by \( (1 + i)^{years} \), where i is the inflation rate, so you can see what your future corpus is worth in today's money.[web:226][web:236]

Why Inflation-Adjusted SIP Planning Matters

Most calculators only show the final corpus number, which can be misleading because rising prices reduce how much that corpus can buy.[web:226][web:239]

By including inflation in every SIP scenario, this tool helps you plan realistically for long-term goals like retirement, children's education, or early financial independence.[web:236]

Standard SIP vs Step-Up SIP

In a standard SIP, your contribution stays constant for the entire tenure, while in a step-up SIP your contribution grows every year by a fixed percentage like 5–10%.[web:231][web:237]

Step-up SIPs better reflect a typical salary path and can meaningfully increase your final corpus without feeling too heavy in the early years.[web:234][web:243]

Goal-Based Reverse SIP Calculation

The goal-based mode takes your desired goal value in today's terms and converts it into a future value using your inflation assumption.[web:235][web:241]

It then uses an iterative calculation to find the monthly SIP needed to hit that target, helping you decide if your goal is affordable or needs adjustment.[web:235]
